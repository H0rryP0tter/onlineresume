A Platform Where Every Career Finds Its Proper Record

OnlineResume presents a new standard for professional credentials — structured, searchable, and worthy of the careers it documents.

In an age of ephemeral digital presence, the professional record has grown fragmented. Credentials scatter across platforms; careers go undocumented; expertise vanishes. OnlineResume proposes a remedy: a single, authoritative ledger of professional lives.

The platform is designed for those whose careers demand more than a bullet-pointed list — journalists who have shaped opinion, academics whose research endures, civil servants whose quiet work sustains institutions.

Each profile is structured as a record of fact: positions held, works published, affiliations maintained. The format owes more to the biographical dictionary than the social network.

No endorsements. No connections. No algorithmic noise. Only the careful accumulation of a professional life, set in type.

Features

Structured for Longevity, Not Virality

Profiles are built around verified facts. Date ranges, institutions, and publication records form the core — not endorsements or connection counts.

Searchable by Field, Institution, and Decade

The index is organized around the structure of professional life itself: discipline, employer, tenure. Find a colleague from 1987 or a specialist in Angolan labour law.

Printable to Archival Standard

Every profile generates a clean, print-ready document formatted to the conventions of academic and legal CVs.
